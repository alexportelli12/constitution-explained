# CHAPTER VI – Parliament

## PART 1: Composition of Parliament

## Article 51. Establishment of Parliament

_Substituted by: LVIII.1974._  
There shall be a Parliament of Malta which shall consist of the President and a House of Representatives.  
_Note: sub-article (4) of this article has been omitted as it has had its effect._  
_The reference to sub-article (4) of article 48 has been omitted in view of the omission of that sub-article._

## Article 52. Composition of the House of Representatives

_Substituted by: LVIII.1974._  
_Amended by: IV.1987; XI.1996; XXI.2007._  
(1) The House of Representatives shall consist of such number of members as corresponds to the total of the electoral divisions as established in accordance with the provisions of article 61(1) of this Constitution and returned to Parliament in accordance with the provisions of sub-article (2) of this article:  
Provided that where –  
a. at a general election, a political party obtains in the aggregate more than fifty per centum of all valid votes cast at that election, as credited to its candidates by the Electoral Commission at the first count of all votes, but the number of elected candidates of that party is less than the total of the elected candidates of all the other parties; or  
b. at a general election contested by more than two political parties, a political party obtains in the aggregate more than forty per centum of all valid votes cast at that election, as credited to its candidates by the Electoral Commission at the first count of all votes, and at least fifty per centum of the first count votes credited to the candidates of the political party obtaining in the aggregate more than fifty per centum of all valid votes cast at that election, and the number of elected candidates of the first-mentioned party is less than the total of the elected candidates of all the other parties,  
the number of members of the House of Representatives shall be increased by such additional members as may be necessary for that first-mentioned party to have one more seat than the total number of seats won by all the other parties taken together. The additional seats so allocated shall be filled from among the candidates of that party who were not elected at that general election, in accordance with the order of votes in excess of fifty per centum of the quota credited to them at the last count during the counting of votes, as credited by the Electoral Commission.  
(2) The members of the House shall be elected by universal and equal suffrage and shall be returned under the single transferable vote system of proportional representation, from electoral divisions in Malta.  
(3) Casual vacancies in the House of Representatives shall be filled as provided by law.  
(4) If any person who is not a member of the House of Representatives is elected to be Speaker of the House, he shall, by virtue of holding the office of Speaker, be a member of the House in addition to the members aforesaid:  
Provided that in the case of a Speaker chosen from outside the House, his membership shall not count for the purposes of the next dissolution of Parliament.

## _Article 52A. Additional seats for under-represented sex_

_Added by: XX.2021._  
(1) If at a general election which is contested by two or more political parties and in which only candidates of two parties are elected (or in the eventuality of an election result as provided for in sub-paragraphs (i) and (ii) of the proviso to sub-article (1) of article 52) and in the event that the number of Members of Parliament of the under-represented sex, elected by virtue of article 52 (inclusive of those members elected at a casual election in terms of the relevant regulations), is less than forty per cent of all the Members of Parliament, then the number of Members of Parliament shall increase by not more than twelve Members of the under-represented sex:  
Provided that the number of Members of Parliament of the under-represented sex elected by virtue of article 52 added to the members of the under-represented sex elected by virtue of this article shall not exceed forty per cent of the total number of elected Members of Parliament making up the House. The candidates elected by virtue of this article are to be apportioned equally between the absolute majority party or the relative majority party and the minority party.  
(2) Such candidate or candidates belonging to the under-represented sex and who are candidates of the party or parties which shall be credited with the additional seats, and which were credited by the Electoral Commission at the last count with the highest or next higher number of votes without being elected, irrespective of the division in which such highest or next higher number of votes occurs, shall be declared by the Electoral Commission as elected to fill the additional seats:  
Provided that in the event that the number of candidates established under this article is not reached, following the application of the preceding sub-article, such candidates belonging to the under-represented sex shall be declared by the Electoral Commission to be elected to fill the remaining additional seats created by this article who, being candidates of the party which is to be credited with the additional seats, were ranked on a national basis by the Electoral Commission with the highest or next higher percentage of the quota obtained on the basis of their respective final count vote before elimination in relation to the quota of the electoral division contested:  
Provided further that in the event that the number of candidates established under this article is not reached as foreseen in this sub-article, the seats thereafter remaining vacant shall be filled by the co-option of a Member or Members by the House and such Member or Members shall be qualified to be elected as a Member of the House as established under article 53, shall not be subject to any disqualifications for membership of the House as provided under article 54 and shall belong to the under-represented sex.  
(3) For the purpose of this article, elected and unelected candidates who are identified as gender-neutral persons in their official documentation, shall be counted with the under-represented sex.  
(4) This article shall remain in force until the expiry of the period of twenty years from the date of its coming into force, unless revoked or re-enacted with or without modification by means of an Act of Parliament according to the provisions of this Constitution.

## Article 53. Qualifications for membership of House of Representatives

(1) Subject to the provisions of subsection (2) of this article, a person shall be qualified to be elected as a member of the House of Representatives if, and shall not be qualified to be so elected unless, he is a citizen of Malta, has attained the age of eighteen years, and has the qualifications for registration as a voter for the election of members of the House of Representatives mentioned in article 57 of this Constitution.  
(2) A person shall not be qualified to be elected as a member of the House of Representatives if –  
a. he is a citizen of a country other than Malta having become such a citizen voluntarily or is under a declaration of allegiance to such a country; or  
b. he is a person declared to be of unsound mind or is detained under the authority of any law relating to mental health; or  
c. he is under sentence of death or is serving a sentence of imprisonment exceeding twelve months, imposed on him by a court of law in any country; or  
d. he is disqualified for membership of the House of Representatives by or under any law for the time being in force in Malta by reason of his having been convicted of any offence relating to elections; or  
e. he is disqualified under the provisions of subsection (3) of this article.  
(3) A person in the public service shall not be qualified to be elected as a member of the House of Representatives or to hold office as a member of that House.  
(4) For the purposes of subsection (3) of this article –  
a. "person in the public service" means any person who holds or is acting in a public office or a contract of service with the Government of Malta or with a local authority;  
b. "public office" has the same meaning as in article 124 of this Constitution and includes an office in the service of a local authority.

## Article 54. Disqualifications for membership of House of Representatives

_Substituted by: LVIII.1974._  
_Amended by: XXXVIII.1976; XXI.2007._  
(1) No person shall be qualified to be elected as a member of the House of Representatives if –  
a. he is a party to, or a partner in a firm or a director or manager of a company which is a party to, any contract with the Government of Malta for or on account of the public service and –  
i. if the contract is for the furnishing or providing of money, not being a contract of loan, goods, services or for the execution of any works, he has not, within one month before the day of the election, published in the Gazette a notice setting out the nature of the contract and his interest, or the interest of any such firm or company, in it; or  
ii. if the contract is for the carrying out of any road transport service, he has not, within one month before the day of the election, published in the Gazette a notice setting out the nature of the contract and the interest of any such firm or company in it; or  
b. he is presently serving in the armed forces of the Government of Malta or the Police Force of Malta; or  
c. he holds or is acting in any public office or is a member of a local government authority; or  
d. he holds or is acting in the office of Judge of the Superior Courts or Magistrate of the Inferior Courts; or  
e. deleted;  
f. he is disqualified for election by any law in force in Malta by reason of his having been convicted of any offence relating to elections; or  
g. he is disqualified for registration as a voter for the election of members of the House of Representatives by or under any law in force in Malta.  
(2) For the purposes of paragraph (a) of subsection (1) of this article, a person shall be deemed to have an interest in a contract with the Government of Malta for or on account of the public service if he is a party to such a contract or a partner in a firm or a director or manager of a company which is a party to such a contract, or if he is otherwise engaged, whether as principal, agent, or sub-contractor, in the work to be done under such contract or in providing goods or services for the carrying out of such contract.

## Article 55. Tenure of office of members

(1) Every member of the House of Representatives shall vacate his seat in the House at the next dissolution of Parliament after his election.  
(2) A member of the House of Representatives shall also vacate his seat in the House –  
a. if he resigns it by writing under his hand addressed to the Speaker, or if the office of Speaker is vacant or the Speaker is absent from Malta, to the Deputy Speaker;  
b. if, in the case of a member of the House who was elected from any electoral division in pursuance of the proviso to subsection (1) of article 52 of this Constitution, the Secretary of the House of Representatives is notified in writing by the Electoral Commission that an additional candidate has been elected in his stead in accordance with that proviso;  
c. if he ceases to be qualified for registration as a voter for the election of members of the House of Representatives;  
d. if any circumstances arise that, if he were not a member of the House, would cause him to be disqualified for election thereto by virtue of paragraphs (a), (b), (c), (d), (f) or (g) of subsection (1) of article 54 of this Constitution; or  
e. in the circumstances specified in subsection (3) of this article.  
(3) If any member of the House of Representatives is absent from the sittings of the House for such period and in such circumstances as may be prescribed in the Standing Orders of the House, the Speaker shall declare his seat vacant:  
Provided that the President, acting in accordance with the advice of the Prime Minister given after the Leader of the Opposition has been consulted, may waive the disqualifications of such member for any period during which such member is suffering from a serious illness.

## Article 56. Voting at elections

(1) Whenever Parliament is dissolved, there shall be a general election of members of the House of Representatives. For the purposes of this article, the expression "general election" means the election of members of the House of Representatives following any dissolution of Parliament.  
(2) Each of the electoral divisions established in accordance with the provisions of article 61 of this Constitution shall return such number of members to the House of Representatives as may be prescribed by any law for the time being in force in Malta, so, however, that –  
a. in the case of a general election, the total number of members to be returned shall be not less than sixty-five;  
b. a candidate shall not be elected as a member of the House of Representatives unless at the general election in consequence of which the House is constituted, he is a candidate for election in the electoral division in which he is registered as a voter;  
c. casual vacancies shall be filled as provided by any law for the time being in force in Malta.  
(3) Where a general election precedes the dissolution of Parliament otherwise than by a period of more than six months, any casual vacancy arising during that period shall not be filled unless the number of vacancies in the House of Representatives exceeds five.

## Article 57. Qualification of voters

_Amended by: LVIII.1974._  
Subject to the provisions of article 58 of this Constitution, a person shall be qualified to be registered as a voter for the election of members of the House of Representatives if, and shall not be qualified to be so registered unless, he is a citizen of Malta, has attained the age of eighteen years and has, under the relevant provisions of any law for the time being in force in Malta, such residence qualifications as may be prescribed by that law.

## Article 58. Disqualification of voters

(1) No person shall be registered as a voter for the election of members of the House of Representatives if he is disqualified for such registration by or under any law for the time being in force in Malta because –  
a. he is adjudged to be of unsound mind;  
b. he is under sentence of death or is serving a sentence of imprisonment (by whatever name called) exceeding twelve months; or  
c. he is disqualified for registration as a voter by any law in force in Malta relating to offences connected with elections.  
(2) Parliament may provide that a person who is registered as a voter in an electoral division and resides in another such division shall, for the purposes of the election of members of the House of Representatives, be deemed to reside in the electoral division in which he is registered as a voter.

## Article 59. Speaker and Deputy Speaker

(1) When the House of Representatives first meets after any general election and before it proceeds to the despatch of any other business, it shall elect a Speaker.  
(2) If the office of Speaker falls vacant at any time before the next dissolution of Parliament, the House of Representatives shall, as soon as practicable, elect another Speaker.  
(3) The Speaker may be elected either –  
a. from among members of the House of Representatives who are not Ministers or Parliamentary Secretaries, or  
b. from among persons who are not members of the House of Representatives:  
Provided that a person who is not a member of the House shall not be elected Speaker if –  
i. he is not a citizen of Malta; or  
ii. he is a person disqualified for election as a member of the House of Representatives by virtue of paragraphs (a), (b), (c), (d), (f) or (g) of sub-article (1) of article 54 of this Constitution.  
(4) When the House of Representatives first meets after any general election, it shall, as soon as practicable, elect a Deputy Speaker from among its members.  
(5) If the office of Deputy Speaker falls vacant at any time before the next dissolution of Parliament, the House of Representatives shall, as soon as convenient, elect another Deputy Speaker from among its members.  
(6) A member of the House of Representatives who is elected to be Speaker or Deputy Speaker shall vacate his seat in the House.  
(7) The Speaker or Deputy Speaker shall vacate his office –  
a. in the case of a Speaker elected from among the members of the House of Representatives or in the case of the Deputy Speaker –  
i. if he ceases to be a member of the House; or  
ii. when the House first meets after any dissolution of Parliament;  
b. in the case of a Speaker elected from among persons who were not members of the House of Representatives –  
i. when the House first meets after any dissolution of Parliament; or  
ii. if any circumstances arise that would cause him to be disqualified for election as a member of the House by virtue of paragraph (a) or paragraph (b) of sub-article (3) of this article;  
c. if he is removed from office by a resolution of the House of Representatives supported by the votes of not less than two-thirds of all the members thereof.  
(8) If, by virtue of subsection (6) of this article, the seat in the House of a member who has been elected Speaker or Deputy Speaker is vacated, the provisions of article 56(2)(c) of this Constitution shall not apply to the election of a member to fill the vacancy resulting from the operation of subsection (6) of this article.  
(9) At any time when the office of Speaker is vacant or the holder of the office is absent from Malta, or is for any reason unable to perform the functions of his office, those functions shall be performed by the Deputy Speaker.  
(10) At any time when both the Speaker and the Deputy Speaker are absent from the House of Representatives, such member of the House as may be elected for that purpose by the House shall preside.  
(11) References in sub-article (8) of this article to subsection (6) of this article shall, in relation to a Speaker who was elected from among persons who were not members of the House of Representatives, be construed as references to subsection (7)(b) of this article.  
(12) The Speaker or Deputy Speaker shall not take part in a vote on any motion in the House of Representatives.

## Article 60. Establishment of Electoral Commission

_Substituted by: LVIII.1974._  
(1) There shall be an Electoral Commission for Malta.  
(2) The Electoral Commission shall consist of a Chairman, who shall be the person for the time being holding the office of Chief Electoral Commissioner, and such number of members not being less than four as the President may from time to time determine.  
(3) The members of the Commission shall be appointed by the President, acting in accordance with the advice of the Prime Minister given after consultation with the Leader of the Opposition, by instrument under the public seal.  
(4) A person shall not be qualified to be appointed as a member of the Commission if he is a Minister, a Parliamentary Secretary, a member of, or a candidate for election to, the House of Representatives, or a public officer.  
(5) Subject to the provisions of this article, a member of the Commission shall vacate his office –  
a. at the expiration of three years from the date of his appointment or at such earlier time as may be specified in the instrument by which he was appointed;  
b. if any circumstances arise that, if he were not a member of the Commission, would cause him to be disqualified for appointment as such.  
(6) Subject to the provisions of subsection (7) of this article, a member of the Commission may be removed from office only for inability to discharge the functions of his office (whether arising from infirmity of body or mind or any other cause) or for misbehaviour, and shall not be removed except in accordance with the provisions of this section.  
(7) A member of the Commission shall be removed from office by the President if the Prime Minister, after consultation with the Leader of the Opposition, represents to the President that the question of removing that member from office under this article ought to be investigated, and the Commission established under article 108(8) of this Constitution recommends to the President that that member ought to be removed from office for inability as aforesaid or for misbehaviour.  
(8) If the office of a member of the Commission is vacant or a member is for any reason unable to perform the functions of his office, the President, acting in accordance with the advice of the Prime Minister given after consultation with the Leader of the Opposition, may appoint a person who is qualified to be appointed as a member of the Commission to act as a member. Any person so appointed shall, subject to the provisions of subsections (5), (6) and (7) of this article, continue so to act until the holder of that office resumes his functions or until the appointment of a new member.  
(9) In the exercise of its functions under the Constitution, the Electoral Commission shall not be subject to the direction or control of any other person or authority.

## Article 61. Electoral divisions

_Amended by: LVIII.1974; XXI.2007._  
(1) The Electoral Commission shall review the boundaries of the electoral divisions into which Malta is divided at intervals of not less than two and not more than five years and may, pursuant to such review, alter such boundaries to such extent as it considers desirable.  
(2) Whenever the boundaries of the electoral divisions are altered in accordance with this article, a general description of the boundaries of the divisions as so altered shall be prepared and submitted to the Prime Minister by the Commission and shall be published in the Gazette.  
(3) Any alteration to the boundaries of the electoral divisions shall come into effect upon the next dissolution of Parliament after the publication of the alterations in the Gazette.

## Article 62. Filling of vacancies

Whenever the seat of a member of the House of Representatives becomes vacant, it shall be filled in accordance with the provisions of any law for the time being in force in Malta.

## Article 63. Determination of questions as to membership

Any question whether –  
a. any person has been validly elected as a member of the House of Representatives;  
b. any member of the House has vacated his seat therein or is required by virtue of the provisions of sub-article (2) of article 55 of this Constitution to cease to perform any of his functions as a member; or  
c. any person has been validly elected as Speaker of the House from among persons who were not members of the House or, having been so elected, has vacated the office of Speaker,  
shall be determined by the Constitutional Court.

## Article 64. Clerk to House of Representatives and his staff

There shall be a Clerk to the House of Representatives and such other staff of the House as may be prescribed by any law for the time being in force in Malta. The Clerk and such other staff shall be public officers.
